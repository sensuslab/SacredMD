 Note: The Tattvartha Sutra was NOT included in the Sacred Books of the East series.
The SBE series (50 volumes, 1879-1910) covered only Vol. 22 (Acaranga + Kalpa Sutras)
and Vol. 45 (Uttaradhyayana + Sutrakritanga Sutras) for Jain texts. The Tattvartha Sutra,
though the most widely read Jain scripture, was not part of SBE. This modern edition by
Vijay K. Jain (2018) with English explanation from Sarvarthasiddhi is the best freely
available complete translation on archive.org.

 Notes 
 The Tattvartha Sutra (also called Tattvarthadhigama Sutra or Mokshashastra) by Acarya
Umasvati/Umasvami is the most authoritative and widely studied Jain text. Composed in
Sanskrit sutra form, it systematically presents the entire Jain philosophical system in
10 chapters covering: faith, knowledge, conduct, the soul, non-soul, influx, bondage,
stoppage, shedding, and liberation.
